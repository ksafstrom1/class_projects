The Early Years of All Star Gyms

By the late 1980s and 1990s, dedicated cheer gyms began to appear. They offered spring floors, tumble tracks, and year-round coaching, which helped the sport grow quickly and safely.

Growing Competition Culture

More competition companies entered the scene and events were held in arenas and convention centers. Teams traveled across states to compete for titles, jackets and bragging rights.

  • Regional and national championships became common.
  • Score sheets broke routines into detailed sections.
  • Divisions were set up by team size and age group.
  • The Cheerleading Worlds debuted in 2004 at Disney World in Florida.

Focus on Safety and Rules

As skills got harder, organizers created rules to control which stunts and tumbling passes were allowed at each level. This lowered risk and kept routines age appropriate.

Coaches used progressions, spotting techniques, and drills so athletes could move up through levels with a strong foundation.
