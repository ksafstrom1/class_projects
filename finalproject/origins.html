The Origins of All Star Cheer

All star cheer has roots in traditional school and college cheerleading. Over time, athletes and coaches wanted a space that focused on skills and performance instead of game day sidelines and crowd leading.

From Sidelines to Spotlight

Early cheerleaders led crowds at football and basketball games. As stunts and tumbling became more advanced, some teams began to compete against each other at local and regional events.

  • Traditional cheer focused on chants and school spirit.
  • Serious coaches and athletes wanted to compete.
  • National organizations started hosting cheer competitions.
  • Independent gyms formed programs to cheer competitively.

The Idea of All Star

The term all star described athletes who trained in a gym instead of a school. These teams focused on routines that combined stunts, tumbling, jumps, and dance in a tight two and a half minute routine on a spring floor.

Skills were grouped by age and difficulty so younger athletes had a way to grow into more advanced levels over time while staying safe.

All star cheer is so popular that countries all over the world are creating programs for their youth and young adults. Each country adds their own “flavor” to the sport.
