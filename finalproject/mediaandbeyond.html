Media and Beyond

All star cheer now lives both on the mat and online. Social platforms, streaming, and documentaries let fans see practices, competitions, and the work that goes into each routine.

Cheer in Video and Streaming

Many events stream live so families and fans can watch from home. Highlight videos show hit routines, upgrades, and behind-the-scenes moments from practice and travel.

Documentaries and series help people outside the sport understand the time, money, and effort that go into a full season.

Social Media and Team Branding

Teams use social media to share announcements, skills, and travel days. Captions, music, and trends keep content fun while still showing the hard work.

  • Short clips of tumbling passes or stunt upgrades.
  • Practice vlogs and travel diaries.
  • Uniform reveals and music previews.

Future of All Star Cheer

The sport continues to grow as new countries form teams and more athletes join at younger ages. Technology, better equipment, and training tools help athletes learn skills safely.

All star cheer continues to evolve with new rules, formats, choreography trends, and a shift toward high-fashion athletic aesthetics.

New Competition Formats

Events are being redesigned to increase excitement and engagement.

  • League 6 now hosts a national points race exclusively for Worlds-eligible Level 6 teams across D1 and D2.
  • The Tournament of Champions (April 2026) brings top teams together for a bracket-style, level-based showdown.
  • The CELEBRATION, powered by The Summit, offers a fun, participation-focused end-of-season event for elite, prep, and rec teams.

Choreography & Performance Trends

Creativity is becoming more important than ever. Judges now reward innovative transitions in pyramids, thematic storytelling, and routines designed around musical cues and dramatic beats. Choreographers are blending styles and working closely with music producers to create more cinematic performances.
