Modern All Star Cheer

Today, all star cheer is a highly organized sport with levels, detailed scoring, and international events. Athletes train like other competitive sports with strength work, flexibility, and conditioning alongside skill practice.

Levels and Divisions

Modern all star cheer uses levels, usually 1 through 7, that group skills from beginner to advanced. Divisions are also separated by age and team size so routines stay fair and safe.

  • Levels 1 and 2 build basics and simple stunts.
  • Levels 3 and 4 add twisting, baskets, and stronger tumbling.
  • Levels 5 and higher include elite pyramids and high-level tumbling.

Score Sheets and Routine Design

Scoresheet Insight

Scoresheet knowledge is so valuable that entire blogs and breakdowns help parents, athletes, coaches, and choreographers understand the fine details. As scoring grows more technical, learning how judges award points has become a major competitive advantage.

View Blog

Score sheets reward difficulty, technique, creativity, and performance. Coaches design routines that balance high-level elements with clean execution.

Panels of judges usually score:

  • Stunts and pyramids.
  • Tumbling and jumps.
  • Dance, timing, and overall impression.

Culture and Community

Modern all star cheer has a strong and loyal community. Athletes often stay in the sport for many years, move into coaching, and follow major events every season both in person and online.
