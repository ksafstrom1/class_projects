What to Do When Tornado Alerts Are Issued

Be Prepared

The best way to stay safe during tornado season is to prepare ahead of time. Know where you’ll go if a tornado warning is issued. Keep a flashlight, weather radio, and first aid kit in your designated shelter area. Pay close attention to local weather alerts so you can act quickly when needed.

It’s also important to wear closed-toe shoes. Tornadoes often occur in warmer months, but flip-flops or sandals can leave you vulnerable to sharp debris such as glass or metal fragments. Consider keeping a sturdy helmet in your shelter, as head injuries from flying debris are a leading cause of tornado-related deaths.

Communication is also critical. Keep a radio, walkie-talkie, or personal alarm with you in case you become trapped or need to signal for help. These tools can make it easier for rescuers to locate you and ensure you can communicate your condition during or after the storm.

During and After the Storm

If a tornado warning is issued, go immediately to a basement or an interior room on the lowest floor. Stay away from windows and doors. In areas such as Tornado Alley, frequent alerts can lead to “warning fatigue,” but it’s important to take every alert seriously.

After the storm passes, be cautious of hazards like broken glass, downed power lines, and unstable structures. Do not leave your shelter until officials announce it is safe to do so. If you’re caught in a vehicle, do not seek shelter under an overpass—this advice is outdated and dangerous. Instead, find a low-lying area such as a ditch and lie flat to minimize your exposure to flying debris.

When it’s safe to do so, check on your neighbors and community members. However, remember to secure your own safety first—just as flight attendants instruct passengers to put on their own oxygen masks before helping others. Protecting yourself ensures that you’ll be in a position to assist those who need help most.

  • Stay in a small, windowless room or basement.
  • Cover yourself with blankets, pillows, or a mattress.
  • Keep a battery-powered radio or weather app nearby.
  • Assist neighbors and pets only when it’s safe to do so.
